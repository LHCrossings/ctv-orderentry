MCO Escalation Guide

CHANNEL FEED OUTAGE PROCEDURES — MASTER CONTROL OPERATIONS

What Counts as an Outage

Begin the escalation process when any of the following occur:

  • Complete signal loss on a channel feed
  • Feed error or stream down affecting broadcast
  • Degraded feed quality impacting air (pixelation, audio dropout, freeze frames)
  • Playout automation failure affecting scheduled programming

Note: Brief glitches under 30 seconds that self-resolve do not require escalation but should still be logged.

Level 1 — Master Control

Steps to take when a channel feed outage is first detected:

  • Assess the feed — identify which channel is affected and the nature of the issue
    • Review monitoring tool: IP Multiviewer (http://34.208.18.64/files/index.html)
    • Check signal path, encoder status, and playout system
  • Attempt to restore — apply known fixes based on the issue type
  • Log the issue immediately in the Outage Report Log (Teams > MC):
    • Time the outage was detected
    • Channel(s) affected
    • Nature of the issue
    • Steps attempted and results
  • Contact Level 2 to notify them of the issue
  • If the feed is restored, complete the log entry and close the incident
  • If the issue is related to Multiplex Cloud Dallas Multiviewer, reach out to confirm and check if they are also experiencing issues on their side
    • Contact: Support@Multiplex-cloud.com
  • If the outage is related to Multiplex feeds, email them to inform them of the issue and request a status update on their end
  • If NOT resolved within 5 minutes of assessment and review — escalate to Level 2
Level 2 — Master Control

Steps when Level 1 has been unable to restore the feed:

  • Review the log and all steps already taken by Level 1
  • Coordinate a fix — apply advanced troubleshooting and coordinate with Level 1 on shift
  • Contact Etere Support if the issue involves playout automation:
    • Etere is the playout automation program used for scheduled programming
    • Have the following ready: time of issue, channel affected, screenshot of error messages or AU logs
    • Contact: support.ai@etere.com
  • Contact Multiplex Support if the issue may be related to their service:
    • Contact: Support@Multiplex-cloud.com
  • Continue logging all new steps and updates in the Outage Report Log
  • Contact Level 3 to notify them of the issue
  • If the feed is restored, complete the log entry and close the incident
  • If NOT resolved within 5 minutes of assessment and review — escalate to Level 3
Level 3 — Operations Command

Final escalation step when Levels 1 and 2 have been unable to restore the feed:

  • Notify management of the issue so they are aware — include:
    • Channel(s) affected
    • Time the outage began
    • All steps already taken by Level 1 and Level 2
    • Current status of the feed
    • Send to: Daniel, Kurt, and the entire MCO Team
  • Call the Director of Operations — contact regardless of time zone
  • Brief the Director of Operations on the full situation: issue, timeline, and all steps taken
  • Continue logging all updates in the Outage Report Log until resolved

Outage Report Log

All incidents — whether resolved at Level 1 or escalated — must be logged.

  • Location: Teams → MC → Outage Report Log
  • Log every incident at the time it occurs — do not wait until resolution
  • Required fields to complete for every entry:
    • Date and time of detection
    • Channel(s) affected
    • Description of the issue
    • Steps taken at each level
    • Time of resolution
    • Root cause (if known)
  • For Level 3 escalations, note the names of all contacts reached and times contacted
